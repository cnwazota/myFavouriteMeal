Ofe Onugbu and Pounded Yam


Origin

Ofe Onugbu (bitter leaf soup) is regarded as having it's origin traced to Nnewi in Anambra State, Nigeria. The Igbos believe Nnewi people introduced the delicious soup to other Igbos from where the delicious consumptiion spread to other parts of Nigeria.
Pounded Yam is a Nigerian food native to the Yoruba, Igbo, Ebira and Tiv ethnic groups. It can be likened to mashed potatoes but heavier and made from boiled yam.

Ingredients

Item Measurement
Onugbu leaves 1 Cup (fresh)
Cow head 2kg
Ground Crayfish 3 tablespoons
Knorr seasoning 2 cubes
Palm oil 2 cooking spoons
Ogiri 1 tablespoon
Dry fish 2 medium sized
Stock fish head 1 big size
Salt and pepper To taste
Pounded cocoa yam 3-4 medium sized
Boiled yam 1 kg
Boiled water 3 litres

Method of Preparation

Onugbu leaves:
To soften the leaves and remove the bitter taste, boil in hot water for 10 to 15 minutes.

Parboil meat with 2 seasoning cubes and half teaspoon of pepper. Allow to boil for 10 miuntes, add water and cook untill meat is tender.

Add hot water, washed dried fish, stockfish and cook until tender.

Add more water, palm oil, ground cray fish, salt and pepper to taste. Stir and allow to boil.

Add the pounded cocoa yam and ogiri. Cook till the cocoa yam dissolves (would likely take about 10 minutes).

Add the onugbu leaves, stir, taste and add more salt, if necessary. Cook for about three minutes.

Serve as desired.

Pounded yam:
Peel the yam and cut into small cubes.

Wash the peeeled yam.

Boil until the yam becomes fork tender.

Pound or blend into a dough-like consistensy until it's completely smooth with no yam chunks left.

Serve as desired.
